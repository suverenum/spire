# Press Release FAQ (PR FAQ) Structure

Based on Amazon's Working Backwards method. Use PR FAQ to define **what** to build and **why** before writing PRDs and specs. If you can't write a compelling press release about the feature, you probably shouldn't build it.

## Philosophy

- **Keep it short** -- max 1 page (A4)
- **Less words, more facts and data**
- **If you have a lot of FAQs then your text isn't good enough**
- Imagine this document will be published tomorrow on TechCrunch

## 1. Heading

Wrap up problem and solution in one line, understandable without reading the doc.

## 2. Subtitle

Key benefits and target audience in one sentence.

## 3. Solution (Key Benefits)

What's the most important customer benefit? Key selling points with screenshots/prototype.

Answer: What's the most important customer benefit in our solution? How do we know this solution will work? (evidence)

## 4. Problem (Target Audience & Evidence)

Who is the customer? What's their problem? How do we know?

Answer:
- Who is the customer? Why is this segment important for us?
- What's the customer problem or opportunity? Why is it important for them?
- How do we know what the customer needs? Do we have evidence?

## 5. Problem (Why Us & Success Metrics)

Why is it interesting for us to solve? How will we measure success?

## 6. Customer Quote

Real or representative customer feedback. Evidence that the solution will work.

## 7. Out of Scope (No-Gos)

What are we NOT doing? Explicitly list excluded features, edge cases, or platforms.

## 8. How to Get Started

Explain how to start using it. What does the experience look like?

## 9. FAQs

Anticipated questions and concise answers. If you have too many, your text isn't clear enough.

---

## The 9 Questions Checklist

Every PR FAQ must answer these with facts and numbers:

### Why? (Problem)

1. **Who is the customer?** Why is this segment important for us?
2. **What's the customer problem or opportunity?** Why is it important for them?
3. **How do we know what the customer needs?** Do we have evidence?
4. **Why is it interesting for us to solve?**
5. **How will we measure success?**

### How? (Solution)

6. **What's the most important customer benefit in our solution?**
7. **How do we know this solution will work?** (evidence)
8. **What's out of scope?** (no-gos)

### What? (Experience)

9. **What does the experience look like?** (screenshots / videos / prototype)

## Approval

- **Two-way door (Type II)** -- can be easily rolled back. Standard review.
- **One-way door (Type I)** -- new big features or breaking changes. Requires sign-off from key stakeholders.

---

## Prioritization (ICE/RICE)

**RICE Score = (Reach x Impact x Confidence) / Effort**

| Factor | Scale | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Impact** | 0-10 | Potential influence on target metric |
| **Confidence** | 0-10 | Amount of evidence (100% = strong proof, 50% = brief data, 25% = moonshot) |
| **Effort** | person-weeks | Total time from all team members |
